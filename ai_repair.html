Ai Guided Repair

What if you had a tool that could identify electronic
parts and look up guides while you focus?

The Problem

First-world countries are producing an abundance of Electronic waste, which is directly threatening the global climate.[7] Therefore, we want to help people repair their electronics, extending lifetime usage and reducing waste. Read more

Our Solution

We have created a tool that can guide and assist you in repairing electronics, so you don't need to throw it away and get a new one.

RelevanceAppropriatenessFeasibility of solution
Norway is #1 when it comes to electronic waste per inhabitant.[6] Norway is good at recycling electronics, but we can make significant improvements when it comes to reparing electronics instead of buying a new one. Our solution is easy to use because of its augmented reality user interface, giving the user a precise indication of the steps required to repair the electronics.

Our solution only requires a phone or our Smart Glasses. 		Training and developing the AI is an iterative process. The AI needs Gigabytes of labled data to be able to understand the different problems electronic devices can have and also how to solve them. The more solutions and devices we add the better the AI will be.

UN SDG 13: Climate Action

We believe that by empowering individuals to repair and construct electronics instead of buying and throwing away, we can contribute to saving the environment. Read more about the United Nations' Sustainable Development Goals here.

Smart Glasses

Glasses have long been a symbol for intelligence, but now we want to literally put intelligence in glasses. A pair of glasses with two front-facing cameras, and laser technology, allows us to capture the reality in front of the usser, and super-impose imagery that's helpful to the wearer when performing repairs.
